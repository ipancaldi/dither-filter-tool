Pancaldi Academy

12 weeks of applied graphic design, brand thinking, digital craft and AI-assisted creative practice.

A practical studio-led immersion for Vicky Pancaldi: not a theoretical course, but a sequence of real briefs, daily critique, visual research, craft exercises and portfolio-building projects.

Course principle

Learn by making. Think by presenting.

Every project follows the same professional process so the rhythm becomes instinctive: understand the problem, make options, review honestly, sharpen the work and explain the idea clearly.

01

Discover

Understand the brief, audience, market and problem.

02

Think

Gather references, define the angle and create a creative hypothesis.

03

Sketch

Generate options before committing to software.

04

Design

Build the strongest idea with craft and discipline.

05

Critique

Review work honestly and identify what is weak.

06

Refine

Improve hierarchy, detail, consistency and clarity.

07

Present

Explain the idea, the process and the final design.

Weekly rhythm

A five-day studio week.

The structure repeats every week, turning research, sketching, production, critique and portfolio documentation into a normal working cadence.

Monday

Brief & Research

Read the brief, define the audience, map references and collect visual evidence.

Tuesday

Sketch & Concept

Build creative territories, thumbnails and early routes before software work begins.

Wednesday

Design & Tools

Move into Figma, Illustrator or Photoshop only when the idea has a direction.

Thursday

Refine & Present

Tighten type, spacing, colour, hierarchy and prepare a coherent presentation.

Friday

Critique & Portfolio

Review the work, note the gaps and archive process for the final case study.

The rhythm repeats so the process becomes instinctive.

Visual energy

The residency should feel like entering a working studio.

Generated imagery gives the plan a more aspirational tone: not classroom graphics, but process walls, dark digital craft, AI direction, critique notes and finished portfolio evidence.

12-week timeline

A practical route from basics to portfolio.

Each week has a theme, a focused skill set, a real-world brief and an output that can either become a case study or feed a stronger final project.

Week 1

Design Fundamentals

Seeing like a designer.

Core skills
  • Hierarchy, contrast, alignment, spacing and visual judgement.
Practical brief
  • Redesign everyday communication pieces.
Output
  • 3 before/after layout studies.
Week 2

Logo & Symbol

Reduction, memory and meaning.

Core skills
  • Sketching, negative space, icon logic and scalability.
Practical brief
  • Create a logo for an independent coffee roastery.
Output
  • Logo system, construction grid, variants and mockups.
Week 3

Typography & Layout

Type as structure and voice.

Core skills
  • Type pairing, grids, rhythm, hierarchy and editorial systems.
Practical brief
  • Design a small cultural magazine or zine.
Output
  • Cover, contents page, 3 spreads and type specimen.
Week 4

Brand Identity

From logo to system.

Core skills
  • Colour, tone of voice, art direction and brand rules.
Practical brief
  • Create a brand for a community charity, food project or creative workshop.
Output
  • Identity system, poster, social templates and brand sheet.
Week 5

Campaign & Poster Series

Conceptual communication.

Core skills
  • Messaging, visual metaphor, composition and image/type relationship.
Practical brief
  • Launch campaign for a local event, exhibition or social cause.
Output
  • 3 poster series, social assets and rationale.
Week 6

Digital Product Basics

UX/UI foundations.

Core skills
  • User journey, information architecture, wireframes and Figma components.
Practical brief
  • Design an app for booking workshops, events or creative classes.
Output
  • User flow, wireframes, high-fidelity screens and clickable prototype.
Week 7

Studio Shadowing at THENERO

With Oriana Gaeta and Marco Grimaldi.

Observation focus
  • Digital art direction, identity, interaction, storytelling, motion, UI craft, case study thinking, client presentation and creative process.
Creative stage
  • No production pressure. Observe, document, ask questions and collect process insights from an agreed observational studio week.
Output
  • Studio observation journal, 10 lessons learned, visual process map and short presentation on how professional work moves from idea to final case study.
Reference
  • thenerodesign.com
Week 8

Digital Experience & Motion

Making brands move.

Core skills
  • Interaction states, scroll rhythm, micro-animation, motion principles and storytelling.
Practical brief
  • Create a landing page for one of the previous brand projects.
Output
  • Responsive homepage design, interaction notes, prototype and motion storyboard.
Week 9

AI as Creative Assistant

Directing AI, not outsourcing taste.

Core skills
  • Prompting, moodboards, image generation, copy iteration, creative territories and critical selection.
Practical brief
  • Create an AI-assisted campaign for a fictional brand.
Output
  • Prompt log, image direction board, campaign visuals and before/after human refinement.
Week 10

Brand Refresh

Strategy, diagnosis and transformation.

Core skills
  • Audit, positioning, redesign logic, visual comparison and presentation.
Practical brief
  • Choose a poorly designed small business and redesign its identity.
Output
  • Brand audit, new identity, before/after presentation and application mockups.
Week 11

Portfolio Case Study Build

Turning work into evidence.

Core skills
  • Storytelling, sequencing, writing rationale, selecting images and editing.
Practical brief
  • Transform all previous work into portfolio case studies.
Output
  • 8 case study pages in a consistent portfolio system.
Week 12

Final Review & Presentation

Present like a designer.

Core skills
  • Critique, confidence, explanation, editing and professional standards.
Practical brief
  • Vicky Pancaldi presents her final portfolio to Igor as Creative Director.
Output
  • Finished portfolio, PDF deck, website or Notion/Readymag-style case study layout.

Tools

Tools are taught only when they are needed.

The tool enters after the design problem is clear. Software supports taste, judgement, process and craft; it does not replace them.

Core design

  • Figma
  • Illustrator
  • Photoshop

Presentation

  • Keynote / Google Slides
  • PDF portfolio
  • Case study writing

AI

  • ChatGPT
  • Image generation
  • Prompt logs
  • AI moodboards
  • Copy and naming exploration

Optional advanced

  • Framer / Webflow
  • After Effects basics
  • Simple HTML/CSS awareness

Taste first.

Tools are not the course. Taste, judgement, process and craft are the course.

How progress is judged

Creative director criteria, not academic grading.

Progress is measured by clarity, control, distinctiveness, polish and whether Vicky Pancaldi can explain the design decisions behind the work.

  • Check

    Is the idea clear?

  • Check

    Is the typography controlled?

  • Check

    Is the hierarchy obvious?

  • Check

    Is the work distinctive?

  • Check

    Is the execution polished?

  • Check

    Can Vicky Pancaldi explain each decision?

  • Check

    Does it look portfolio-worthy?

  • Check

    Did AI improve the work or just decorate it?
